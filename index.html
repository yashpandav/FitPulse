Understanding the Modern Lifestyle

   Living in a world filled with constant demands and deadlines, stress has become a familiar companion. Balancing work, family, and personal responsibilities can often lead to heightened stress levels, affecting both our mental and physical health.
   With the hustle and bustle of modern life, it's not uncommon to find ourselves burning the midnight oil or sacrificing precious hours of sleep. Late nights and early mornings can disrupt our circadian rhythm, impacting overall health and vitality.
   Socializing is an integral part of our lives, and celebrations and gatherings contribute to our social well-being. However, excessive partying and late-night events may tip the balance, affecting our sleep patterns and overall health.
   In a world where time is of the essence, fast food and processed snacks often find their way into our daily diets. While convenient, these choices may lack the essential nutrients our bodies need, contributing to various health concerns.

Depression

Depression affects about 1 in 15 adults in any given year, and 1 in 6 people will experience depression at some time in their life. An Our World In Data study estimates about 3.4% (2-6% when including the margin of error) of the global population has depression. This is about 264 million people worldwide.
